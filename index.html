Methodology
How to read this map

This tracker aggregates incident-level records from independent monitoring datasets covering Israeli military operations and settler violence directed at Palestinian civilians in the West Bank, including East Jerusalem.

Records are filtered to retain only events where the perpetrator is identified as Israeli military, settlers, Israeli police, settlement emergency squads, or private Israeli security — and the affected party is Palestinian civilians. Incidents involving Palestinian armed groups, intra-Palestinian disputes, or protest activity are excluded; those belong to separate tracking efforts.

Coordinates from ACLED reflect the location where the incident occurred. Records from the Masafer Yatta documentation project use village-level coordinates with minimal jitter applied so co-located events render distinguishably on the map; these points should be read as "incident occurred in this village" rather than at an exact GPS coordinate.

Gaps and undercount are real. Many incidents — particularly in remote herding communities and during periods of communication blackout — are never documented. The numbers here are a floor, not a ceiling.
